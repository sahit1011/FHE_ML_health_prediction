How Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) Protects Your Privacy

Our health prediction service uses cutting-edge Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) technology to ensure your sensitive health data remains private while still providing accurate predictions.

The Privacy Challenge in Health Predictions

Traditional machine learning services require access to your raw data to make predictions. This creates privacy concerns:

  • Your sensitive health information is exposed to the service provider
  • Data breaches could expose your personal health information
  • Your data might be used for purposes beyond what you consented to
  • Regulatory compliance (like HIPAA) becomes more complex
Traditional Approach: Your Data is Exposed

In traditional cloud-based health prediction services, your unencrypted health data is sent to the server, processed in plaintext, and then results are returned to you. At multiple points, your sensitive information is exposed.

What is Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)?

Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) is a revolutionary encryption technology that allows computations to be performed directly on encrypted data without ever decrypting it.

Key Features of FHE:
  • Encrypted Computation: Perform calculations on encrypted data without decryption
  • End-to-End Encryption: Data remains encrypted throughout the entire process
  • Mathematical Guarantee: Privacy is protected by mathematical properties, not just policies
  • Zero Knowledge: The server learns nothing about your data
FHE Analogy: The Locked Box

Imagine placing your health data in a special locked box. You keep the only key. The box has magical properties that allow others to perform calculations on what's inside without ever opening the box or seeing its contents. When the calculations are complete, only you can unlock the box to see the results.

FHE Approach: Your Data Stays Private

With FHE, your health data is encrypted on your device before being sent to the server. The server performs all calculations on the encrypted data and returns encrypted results. Only you can decrypt these results. The server never sees your actual health information.

How Our FHE Health Prediction Works

The FHE Health Prediction Process
Step 1: Encryption

Your health data is encrypted on your device using your unique encryption key

Step 2: Encrypted Processing

The server processes your encrypted data using FHE techniques

Step 3: Decryption

Encrypted results are sent back and decrypted only on your device

Technical Details

Our implementation uses the CKKS (Cheon-Kim-Kim-Song) encryption scheme, which is specifically designed for approximate arithmetic on encrypted real numbers. This makes it ideal for machine learning applications.

Client-Side Operations
  1. Generate encryption keys (public and private)
  2. Encrypt health metrics using the public key
  3. Send only the encrypted data to the server
  4. Receive encrypted prediction results
  5. Decrypt results using the private key
Server-Side Operations
  1. Receive encrypted health data
  2. Process data through an FHE-compatible machine learning model
  3. Perform all calculations on encrypted data
  4. Generate encrypted prediction results
  5. Return encrypted results to the client
Important Privacy Guarantee

At no point during this process does the server have access to your unencrypted health data or the means to decrypt it. The mathematical properties of FHE ensure that even the operators of the service cannot access your private information.

Limitations and Considerations

While FHE provides powerful privacy guarantees, it's important to understand its current limitations:

Performance Impact

FHE operations are computationally intensive and can be slower than plaintext calculations. Our implementation is optimized for health predictions, but you may notice slightly longer processing times compared to non-private alternatives.

Model Complexity

Not all machine learning models can be efficiently implemented with FHE. We've carefully designed our health prediction model to work well with FHE while maintaining high accuracy.
